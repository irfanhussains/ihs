Medical Coding

The diagnostic and procedure codes are derived from medical paperwork and records, including laboratory and radiologic results, transcriptions of doctors' notes, and other data. An alphanumeric system called medical coding facilitates the development and transformation of medical diagnoses, treatments, services, and equipment. IHS's coding staff is extremely skilled, and above importantly, we have tools that allow us to complete documentation without making mistakes.

Electronic claims submission

Denial Management

Denial management is a tactical procedure that aids in resolving problems with medical claim denials. The procedure also reduces the likelihood of upcoming rejections.

The IMMP process, or identity, manage, monitor, and prevent, is the responsibility of our denial management team.

The mismatch of codes is a common cause of claim denial; however, other minor flaws can also contribute to a claim being denied.

However, if your denial management staff is skilled and your system is effective, you may be able to act as a barrier to stop the technical justification of false claims.

Authorization

Pre-Authorization and prior authorization are two distinct needs for every medical operation, particularly when it comes to surgeries or other treatments where expensive medical gadgets may be used and might be pricey for any patient. There is always a danger that claims will be denied without adequate prior permission.

When a therapy or diagnostic may not be funded by an insurance company, a prior permission is required. We always choose practice management systems, nevertheless. The American Medical Association (AMA) asserts that prior permission is problematic because it may delay or interrupt patient treatment, according to research done by its concern centers.

Services we provide to Radiology Centers

Due to our expertise working with numerous radiology centers, we are aware of the particular difficulties and needs of the industry. In order to ensure proper reimbursement and effective revenue cycle management, our devoted team of professionals is committed to offering radiology centers specifically designed medical billing services that meet their unique demands.

Coding and Documentation

To support the services rendered, radiology operations need precise coding and thorough documentation. To guarantee accurate billing and reimbursement, skilled medical coders analyze the radiological data and assign the right codes.

Claims Generation

Radiology facilities produce claims using the coded data. These claims include pertinent information about the patient and the provider and any necessary procedure or diagnostic codes and supporting paperwork.

Before offering radiological treatments, our centers frequently confirm the patient's insurance coverage and acquire the required authorizations. This procedure minimizes claim denials and guarantees that the supplied services are eligible for payment.

Claim Submission and Follow-Up

Depending on the needs of the payer, radiology centers send claims to IHS online or through paper-based procedures. They keep a careful eye on the claims' progress and carry out follow-up tasks to address any problems or discrepancies and guarantee prompt payment.

Reimbursement and Payment Posting

Following the processing and approval of the claims, radiology centers are paid by IHS. After balancing the received payments with the submitted claims, they correctly record the payments in their billing systems.

Revenue Cycle Management: We provide radiology centers with full revenue cycle management services, such as precise charge recording, swift payment posting, and proactive rejection management. The billing procedure is streamlined, income is maximized, and financial disturbances are decreased as a result.

Technology Integration

IHS uses cutting-edge medical billing software and technological solutions, which include integration with PACS and RIS (radiology information systems). This connection guarantees smooth data flow and improves the management of radiology billing procedures.
